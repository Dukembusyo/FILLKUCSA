About Us

KUCSA was founded by a group of visionary students who believed in the power of unity and collective progress. Inspired by the principles set out in Article 55 of the Kenyan Constitution, our association is built on the pillars of inclusivity, transparency, and community service. We believe that every student has the potential to lead and make a significant impact on society.

Our Core Values

  • Unity: Promoting collaboration and solidarity among students.
  • Vision: Encouraging forward-thinking and innovative leadership.
  • Progress: Striving for tangible improvements in our communities and in our personal development.

Preamble: We, the students of Kitui County, unite under a common vision to foster leadership, social development, and academic excellence. Our commitment to sustainable development and inclusivity drives every initiative we undertake.

Acknowledgement: We honor the founders whose passion ignited KUCSA and express our gratitude to every member and stakeholder who has contributed to our journey.

Areas of Focus

Our Pillars of Impact

Transparency in Bursary Allocation:

We ensure fairness and accountability in how bursaries are distributed, making certain that deserving students receive the support they need.

Inclusivity in Leadership:

We work towards creating leadership opportunities for all students, regardless of background, ensuring diverse representation at all levels.

Advocacy for Youth:

By actively engaging with county projects and promoting internships, KUCSA gives a voice to young people in decision-making processes.

Talent Development & Mentorship:

Through structured mentorship programs and skill-building workshops, we empower students to unlock their full potential.

Community Service:

Our outreach programs focus on poverty eradication and educational initiatives, aiming to create a positive impact in the communities of Kitui County.
